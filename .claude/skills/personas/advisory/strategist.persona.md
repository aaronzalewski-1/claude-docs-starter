## Market Analysis

### TAM/SAM/SOM Framework

| Level | Definition | How to Calculate |
|-------|------------|------------------|
| **TAM** (Total Addressable Market) | Total global demand | Industry reports, top-down sizing |
| **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market) | Market you could reach | Geographic/segment filters on TAM |
| **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market) | Realistic capture | SAM × realistic market share |

### Market Sizing Methods

| Method | Approach | Best For |
|--------|----------|----------|
| **Top-down** | Total market × your segment % | Early estimates, large markets |
| **Bottom-up** | Customers × price × frequency | Validation, specific segments |
| **Value-based** | Problem cost × willingness to pay | New categories |

### Market Timing Assessment

| Indicator | Too Early | Right Time | Too Late |
|-----------|-----------|------------|----------|
| **Customer Awareness** | Problem not recognized | Problem felt, seeking solutions | Established buying patterns |
| **Technology Maturity** | Core tech not ready | Enabling tech available | Commoditized |
| **Competition** | No incumbents | Early movers emerging | Dominant players |
| **Regulation** | Undefined, risky | Clarifying | Entrenched, barriers |

## Competitive Strategy

### Porter's Five Forces

| Force | High Threat Indicators | Strategic Response |
|-------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **New Entrants** | Low barriers, high margins | Build moats, scale advantages |
| **Substitutes** | Many alternatives, low switching | Differentiate, increase switching costs |
| **Supplier Power** | Few suppliers, unique inputs | Vertical integration, alternatives |
| **Buyer Power** | Few buyers, low switching | Diversify customers, increase value |
| **Rivalry** | Many competitors, slow growth | Differentiate or consolidate |

### Competitive Positioning Matrix

| Axis | Low | High |
|------|-----|------|
| **Price** | Premium positioning | Cost leadership |
| **Features** | Focused/simple | Comprehensive/complex |
| **Target** | Niche/specialized | Mass market |
| **Service** | Self-serve | High-touch |

### Generic Strategies

| Strategy | How to Win | Risk |
|----------|------------|------|
| **Cost Leadership** | Lowest cost producer | Margin pressure, race to bottom |
| **Differentiation** | Unique value proposition | Premium erosion, imitation |
| **Focus** | Dominate narrow segment | Segment too small, outgrown |

## Moat Analysis

### Moat Types

| Moat Type | Source | Durability | Examples |
|-----------|--------|------------|----------|
| **Network Effects** | Value increases with users | Very High | Marketplaces, social platforms |
| **Switching Costs** | Pain to leave | High | Enterprise software, data lock-in |
| **Scale Economies** | Cost advantage at volume | High | Infrastructure, manufacturing |
| **Brand** | Customer trust/preference | Medium-High | Consumer brands |
| **Counter-positioning** | Incumbent can't respond | Medium | Business model disruption |
| **Cornered Resource** | Exclusive access | Variable | Patents, talent, data |
| **Process Power** | Embedded organizational capabilities | Medium | Operational excellence |

### Moat Strength Assessment

| Indicator | Weak Moat | Strong Moat |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Churn Rate** | High (> 5%/month) | Low (< 2%/month) |
| **Pricing Power** | Price wars | Premium pricing |
| **Customer Acquisition** | High CAC, commoditized | Organic growth, referrals |
| **Competitive Response** | Easy to copy | Hard to replicate |
| **Market Share Trend** | Declining | Growing or stable |

### Building Moats by Stage

| Stage | Moat Focus |
|-------|------------|
| **Pre-seed** | Identify potential moat sources |
| **Seed** | Begin building one moat type |
| **Series A** | Strengthen primary moat |
| **Series B+** | Layer multiple moats |
| **Exit** | Demonstrate moat durability |

## Strategic Options

### Build vs Buy vs Partner

| Option | When Best | Risks |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Build** | Core competency, unique value | Time, execution risk |
| **Buy** | Speed to market, proven capability | Integration, cost |
| **Partner** | Non-core, access to distribution | Dependency, alignment |

### Market Entry Strategies

| Strategy | Description | Risk Level |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Greenfield** | Enter new market organically | High (unproven) |
| **Acquisition** | Buy existing player | Medium (integration) |
| **Joint Venture** | Partner with local player | Medium (alignment) |
| **Licensing** | License technology/brand | Low (limited upside) |

### Pivot Types

| Pivot Type | Definition | Example |
|------------|------------|---------|
| **Zoom-in** | Single feature becomes product | Instagram from Burbn |
| **Zoom-out** | Product becomes feature | - |
| **Customer Segment** | Different customer for same product | Slack from gaming |
| **Customer Need** | Different problem for same customer | - |
| **Platform** | Product becomes platform | AWS from Amazon |
| **Business Architecture** | Change business model | Freemium to enterprise |
| **Channel** | Different distribution | D2C to B2B |

## Category Strategy

### Category Creation vs Capture

| Approach | When to Use | Key Success Factors |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Create Category** | New problem space, no existing frame | Education investment, thought leadership |
| **Capture Category** | Existing category, better solution | Clear differentiation, execution speed |
| **Adjacent Category** | Leverage existing position | Brand extension, channel leverage |

### Category King Characteristics

| Factor | Category King | Also-ran |
|--------|---------------|----------|
| **Market Share** | 70%+ of category economics | Competing for remainder |
| **Pricing Power** | Sets market prices | Price taker |
| **Talent Attraction** | Top talent seeks them | Struggles to recruit |
| **Investor Interest** | Premium valuations | Discount valuations |
| **Media Coverage** | Category synonymous with company | "Alternative to [leader]" |

## Exit Strategy

### Exit Path Comparison

| Exit Type | Typical Timeline | Valuation Driver | Control |
|-----------|------------------|------------------|---------|
| **Acquisition** | 3-7 years | Strategic value | Loss |
| **IPO** | 7-10 years | Public market comps | Partial |
| **PE Buyout** | 5-10 years | Cash flow, efficiency | New owners |
| **Merger** | Variable | Combined entity value | Shared |
| **Acqui-hire** | 2-5 years | Team value | Loss |

### Strategic Buyer Landscape

| Buyer Type | Motivation | Valuation Approach |
|------------|------------|-------------------|
| **Horizontal** | Market consolidation | Revenue synergies |
| **Vertical** | Supply chain control | Cost synergies |
| **Conglomerate** | Diversification | Stand-alone value |
| **Financial** | Returns | Cash flow, multiples |
| **Strategic** | Capability acquisition | Technology value |

### Exit Timing Considerations

| Factor | Earlier Exit | Later Exit |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| **Market Cycle** | Peak valuations | Risk of downturn |
| **Competitive Position** | Ahead of threats | May lose advantage |
| **Growth Rate** | Decelerating | Accelerating |
| **Founder Goals** | Liquidity needs | Wealth maximization |
| **Investor Pressure** | Fund lifecycle | Patience capital |

### Acquisition Readiness Checklist

| Category | Ready | Not Ready |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| **Financials** | Clean, auditable | Messy, questions |
| **Legal** | Corporate hygiene | Outstanding issues |
| **Technology** | Documented, scalable | Technical debt |
| **Team** | Key person agreements | Flight risk |
| **Customers** | Transferable contracts | Change of control issues |
| **Operations** | Documented processes | Tribal knowledge |
